Albin Salazar, PhD Candidate

I am a Theoretical Computer Science PhD candidate in the Antique team hosted at ENS-PSL and Inria-Paris and under the supervision of Jérôme Feret. My broad research interest is on leveraging static analysis techniques to develop faithful models in biology. I am interested on a diverse range of topics such as multi-scale systems representation, model reduction techniques, verification of machine learning methods to concentration- and time-scale separation in biology, cancer signalling networks, and personalised and precision medicine.

Prior to my PhD, I obtained a masters in Quantitative Biology from CRI-Paris, where I completed a variety of research experiences in experimental and theoretical biology. Additionally, I hold a bachelors degree in Chemistry from Southern Connecticut State University and where I contributed to research developing ab initio methods.

Learning by doing.

I am heavily committed to the practice of teaching, and believe that any person can contribute to (open) research. Consequently I co-founded Home Association, a non-profit orginization that seeks to implement project-based activities for University students such that the goal is to learn and develop skills. The theme of the projects encompasses the sustainable development goals (SDGs) and is context specific: I have taught in Ghana (2018), Rwanda (2019-present), and (soon) Costa Rica. My contribututions to teaching is programming, open hard-ware technology (e.g., arduino boards), and human-centered design.
